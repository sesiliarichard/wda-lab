Founder & CEO

Founder & CEO — WDA Lab

Julius

A graduate of the College of African WildData Management with a degree in WildData Management, Julius's career has been shaped by years of hands-on involvement in field research and conservation data across different localities.

His core expertise spans data collection, entry, annotation, and analysis — skills he has applied across a diverse range of environments and ecosystems. Over 2 years, Julius has collaborated with researchers and organizations on some of Africa's most significant WildData surveys.

His project experience includes the KAZA transboundary conservation initiative in Botswana, the Save Valley Conservancy project in Zimbabwe, the Boma-Badingilo ecosystem survey in South Sudan, and a research project with the Tanzania WildData Research Institute in Katavi National Park.

Closer to home, Julius has worked as a Research Assistant with Wild-Co Tanzania, contributing to data collection efforts in the iconic Serengeti National Park. Based in Arusha, Tanzania, Julius founded WDA Lab with a clear purpose — to bridge the gap between raw WildData data and actionable conservation intelligence.

Operations Manager & WildData Specialist

Operations Manager & WildData Specialist — WDA Lab

Wenceslaus

Wenceslaus is a WildData data annotation professional holding a Bachelor degree of Science in WildData Management with strong experience supporting national WildData monitoring and conservation initiatives in Tanzania.

He has worked with the Centre for Research Cooperation (CRC) under the Modernization of WildData Survey Project and also contributed to TAWIRI in the Conservation Information and Monitoring Section, supporting WildData survey data annotation for national censuses and conservation planning.

He oversees daily annotation lab operations by coordinating annotators, ensuring annotation quality, managing schedules, and preparing monthly income and expense reports.
